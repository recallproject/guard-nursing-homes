Step 12: v5 Research-Backed Improvements Claude Code

Research-driven changes based on UX trust studies, typography contrast research, and conversion optimization.

HERO HEADLINE: Change from "Search any nursing home in America" to
"Every nursing home has a record. Here's how to read it."
Research: Benefit-driven headlines outperform descriptive ones. The old
headline was functional but didn't communicate value proposition.
Alternative candidates if A/B testing:
- "The data CMS doesn't make easy to find."
- "Before you choose a nursing home, check the record."
- "14,713 nursing homes. 18 databases. One report."

HERO SUB TEXT: Change color from var(--muted) #64748b to var(--text)
#334155. This improves contrast ratio from ~4.5:1 to ~7.5:1 against
#edf1f7 background. WCAG AAA compliance. Research: low-contrast body
text is the #1 readability complaint on the web.

STATS STRIP: Convert from single card to 3 separate cards with Style D
treatment — individual shadows, 4px accent bars (navy gradient for
14,713 and 18, orange gradient for $492M). Cards hover with
translateY(-2px). Gap: 16px. This follows card UI best practices —
visual separation helps users focus on each stat independently.

HOW IT WORKS: Convert from dark (#111827) to light (white card with
shadow-lg + accent bar). Research: An eye tracking study (ACM ETRA
2025) and an e-commerce trust study both found light backgrounds score
higher for trust and readability. Dark sections reduce perceived
credibility for data-heavy products. The gradient accent bar at top
maintains visual interest without the trust penalty.

FACILITY CARD CTAs: Add a "See full report for [Name] →" link at the
bottom of each facility card in the "Know Before You Choose" section.
border-top separator, centered, blue text. On hover: fill blue
background with white text. This gives users a clear click target —
the cards themselves are clickable but the CTA makes intent explicit.
Green hover variant for TOP PERFORMER cards.
