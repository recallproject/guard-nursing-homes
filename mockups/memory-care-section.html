Sunny Acres Nursing & Rehabilitation Center

Memory Care Safety

CMS MDS Quality Measures · Q3 2025
How this facility treats residents with dementia and cognitive impairment

Elevated Antipsychotic Use

This facility medicates 28.3% of long-stay residents with antipsychotic drugs — nearly double the national average of 14.9%. Antipsychotics are sometimes used to sedate residents with dementia rather than provide appropriate care. CMS has flagged this practice as a quality concern.

Antipsychotic medication
28.3% vs 14.9% avg
What this means: 28.3% of long-stay residents at this facility are receiving antipsychotic medication. CMS already excludes residents diagnosed with schizophrenia, Huntington's disease, and Tourette syndrome — so this number represents residents without those conditions. High rates may indicate the facility is using medication to manage behavior rather than providing person-centered dementia care.
Physical restraints
0.0% vs 0.4% avg
What this means: No long-stay residents are physically restrained (vests, mitts, belts, or side rails used as restraints). CMS has driven restraint use to near-zero nationally. Any facility above 1% should raise serious questions.
Falls with major injury
4.1% vs 3.4% avg
What this means: 4.1% of long-stay residents experienced a fall that caused a fracture, head injury, or other major harm. Residents with dementia are at higher risk for falls. Rates above the national average may indicate inadequate supervision, staffing, or fall prevention measures.
Depressive symptoms
18.2% vs 22.0% avg
What this means: 18.2% of long-stay residents show moderate to severe depressive symptoms. Depression is common in dementia patients but often goes undiagnosed. A lower-than-average rate can mean better screening and treatment — or under-reporting. Consider this alongside other indicators.
Significant weight loss
9.8% vs 7.1% avg
What this means: 9.8% of long-stay residents have experienced significant unplanned weight loss. Residents with dementia often have difficulty eating or forget meals. Elevated rates can indicate inadequate feeding assistance, meal supervision, or nutritional monitoring.
Quality measure data is from CMS's Minimum Data Set (MDS), reported quarterly. Antipsychotic rates exclude residents with schizophrenia, Huntington's disease, and Tourette syndrome per CMS methodology. A high rate does not automatically indicate improper care but warrants further investigation. Learn more about our methodology. Verify on CMS.gov →
